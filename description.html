To Mask or Not To Mask? Balancing Privacy with Visual Confirmation Utility in
Activity-Oriented Wearable Cameras

Alharbi R, Tolba M, Petito LC, Hester J, Alshurafa N

Activity-oriented cameras are used to provide fine-grained visual confirmation of specific hand-related activities in real
world settings. Recent studies have shown that bystander privacy concerns limit participant willingness to wear a camera.

Researchers have investigated different obfuscation methods as an approach to enhance bystander
privacy; however, these methods may have varying effects on the visual confirmation utility of the image.

We wanted to test the effects of an activity-oriented partial obfuscation approach with varying degrees of obfuscation on bystander privacy and visual confirmation utility.

Research Questions

  1. How do different activity-oriented partial obfuscation methods affect the utility of identifying hand-to-head activities by a human viewer?
    Measure: The wearer activity
  2. How do different activity-oriented partial obfuscation methods affect bystander privacy and concerns?
    Measure: The bystander activity
  3. Which of the five obfuscation methods tested achieves greatest visual confirmation utility and bystander privacy while reducing concerns
    when collecting hand-to-head activities and why?
